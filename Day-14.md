## Chapter Summary
In this chapter, James Clear explains how **commitment devices** and **environment design** can make good habits inevitable and bad habits nearly impossible. By locking in future behavior, we reduce reliance on willpower.

## Key Ideas
- Commitment devices restrict future choices.
- Making bad habits difficult reduces their occurrence.
- Designing the environment enforces good decisions.
- Long-term habits require short-term structure.

## Key Takeaways
- Use commitment devices to stay consistent.
- Increase friction for bad habits.
- Reduce friction for good habits.
- Discipline can be automated through systems.
