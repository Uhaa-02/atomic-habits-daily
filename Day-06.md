# Day 06 – Atomic Habits 

## Chapter Summary
This chapter focuses on the **first law of behavior change: Make it obvious**. It explains how designing your environment and setting clear cues can make good habits easier and bad habits harder.

## Key Ideas
- The first law of behavior change is **Make it obvious**.
- Habits are easier to build when cues are visible.
- Environment plays a bigger role than motivation.
- Behavior change begins with awareness.

## Key Takeaways
- You don’t rise to the level of your goals; you fall to the level of your systems.
- Making cues obvious increases the chances of habit formation.
- Small changes in surroundings can lead to consistent habits.

## Personal Reflection
Today’s reading made me realize that my environment strongly influences my actions. Instead of relying on willpower, I can design my surroundings to support better habits.

## Action Step
I will make one positive habit obvious today by placing a clear visual cue in my environment.

## Favorite Line
> “You don’t have to be aware of the cue for a habit to begin, but you must be aware of the habit if you want to change it.”
