## Chapter Summary
In this chapter, James Clear explains how **accountability** increases the likelihood of sticking to habits. When our actions are visible to others, we feel social pressure to stay consistent and follow through.

## Key Ideas
- Accountability adds external pressure to habits.
- People are more consistent when expectations are clear.
- Social consequences influence behavior.
- Commitment increases when habits are shared.

## Key Takeaways
- An accountability partner helps maintain discipline.
- Public commitments increase follow-through.
- Social pressure can reinforce good habits.
- Responsibility encourages consistency.

## Personal Reflection
This chapter showed me that relying only on self-discipline can be difficult. Having someone to check in with can motivate me to stay consistent and avoid skipping habits.

## Action Step
I will share one habit goal with a friend or family member and ask them to hold me accountable.
