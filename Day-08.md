## Chapter Summary - MOTIVATION IS OVERRATED; Environment Often Matters More
This chapter focuses on designing your environment to make good habits **obvious and easy**. James Clear explains that behavior is strongly influenced by surroundings, and small environmental changes can lead to big habit improvements.

## Key Ideas
- Environment is more powerful than motivation.
- Visual cues influence behavior more than self-control.
- Making good habits visible increases consistency.
- Reducing friction helps habits stick.

## Key Takeaways
- Design your environment to support good habits.
- Make positive cues visible and accessible.
- Remove or hide cues that trigger bad habits.
- Small environmental changes can create lasting habits.
